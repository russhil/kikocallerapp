# Kiko AI — New Feature Brief (for Landing Page Design)

**Product context (for the designer):** Kiko AI is an Android app for small Indian retail shop owners (kirana / general stores, restaurants, etc.). It automatically turns phone-call orders into clean, structured digital orders using AI — no typing, no notebooks. The audience is non-technical shopkeepers, many of whom read Hindi or Gujarati more comfortably than English. Tone: simple, reassuring, "made for your shop." Visual language: friendly, trustworthy, mobile-first (the product is a phone app), indigo + cyan brand palette.

Two new features to feature on the landing page:

---

## FEATURE 1 — View the whole app in your language (English · हिंदी · ગુજરાતી)

### One-line
"Use Kiko in the language you think in — English, Hindi, or Gujarati."

### What it is
Kiko now runs **fully in three languages**: English, Hindi (हिंदी), and Gujarati (ગુજરાતી). This isn't just menu labels — **everything** adapts: every screen, button, notification, and even the **order details themselves** (product names, customer notes, delivery address) are shown in the language the shopkeeper chooses. A Gujarati-only shopkeeper can run the entire business end-to-end without seeing a word of English.

### Why it matters (benefit)
- Shopkeepers understand their orders at a glance — no guessing, no errors from reading an unfamiliar script.
- Removes the #1 barrier for non-English users adopting a "tech" app.
- Builds trust: the app speaks *their* language, literally.

### Key capabilities to highlight
1. **Pick your language the moment you open the app** — a friendly first-launch screen with three big choices (English / हिंदी / ગુજરાતી). No setup, no confusion.
2. **Change anytime** — one tap in Settings switches the entire app instantly.
3. **Orders translate too, not just the app** — the AI-extracted order (items, quantities, notes, address) appears in the chosen language. e.g. an order shows "બટર પરાઠા – 3" in Gujarati or "बटर पराठा – 3" in Hindi.
4. **Call transcripts in your language** — the converted text of the call is readable in the chosen language.
5. **Future orders come ready in your language** automatically; older orders translate on the fly.

### How it works (3 simple steps — good for a "how it works" strip)
1. Open Kiko → choose your language.
2. Take orders over the phone like normal.
3. See every order — and the whole app — in your language.

### Suggested visuals
- **Hero device mockup trio**: the same Kiko order screen shown three times side-by-side, one in English, one in Hindi, one in Gujarati (identical layout, different script) — instantly communicates "same app, your language."
- A language-picker screen mockup (three tappable cards: English / हिंदी / ગુજરાતી).
- Small flag-free language chips (English · हिंदी · ગુજરાતી) as a recurring motif.

### Sample copy / headlines the agent can use
- Headline: **"Your shop. Your language."**
- Subhead: "Kiko works fully in English, Hindi and Gujarati — orders, screens, everything."
- Microcopy: "Switch anytime. Nothing to learn."

## FEATURE 2 — Download or share order reports (PDF & Excel)

### One-line
"Turn your call orders into a clean report — download it or share on WhatsApp in one tap."

### What it is
Shopkeepers can **export their orders as a professional PDF or an Excel sheet**. They can **save the file to their phone's Downloads** to keep records, or **share it instantly** (WhatsApp, email, etc.) with a supplier, accountant, or family member. The report includes order ID, date, customer, phone, items, total, and status — and it's generated **in the shopkeeper's chosen language**.

### Why it matters (benefit)
- Easy record-keeping and accounting without writing anything down.
- Share the day's / week's orders with a delivery person, accountant, or supplier in seconds.
- Excel for anyone who wants to total up sales or import into their own books; PDF for a clean, printable summary.

### Key capabilities to highlight
1. **Two formats:** **PDF** (clean, printable summary) and **Excel** (sortable spreadsheet of all orders).
2. **Pick exactly what to export** — filter by a **date range** (today, last 7 days, this month, or a custom range) and/or search, then export only those orders.
3. **Save to phone (Download)** — the file lands in the device's Downloads folder for safekeeping.
4. **Share anywhere** — one tap opens the share sheet (WhatsApp, email, Drive…).
5. **Reports in your language** — column headings and order details print in English, Hindi, or Gujarati to match the app.

### How it works (3 simple steps)
1. Open your orders → (optional) pick a date range.
2. Tap Export → choose **PDF** or **Excel**.
3. Save to your phone or share on WhatsApp — done.

### Suggested visuals
- A phone showing the orders list with a **date-range chip** ("This month") and an **export button** highlighted.
- A small "save success" toast/card: "Saved to Downloads" + a Share button.
- A flat-lay of the **generated PDF report** (a tidy table) and an **Excel sheet** next to the phone, both in Gujarati/Hindi to reinforce Feature 1.
- WhatsApp share icon to make "share in one tap" obvious.

### Sample copy / headlines
- Headline: **"Your orders, ready to share."**
- Subhead: "Export any day's orders as a PDF or Excel — download it or send it on WhatsApp."
- Microcopy: "Filter by date. Export. Done."
